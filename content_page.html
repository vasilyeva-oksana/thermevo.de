The Swiss Alps will see 380-meter skycraper, Tokio has 4-kilometer building. Is the future coming or is it just a fantasy of architects who work for PRs of corporations?

380-meter superhotel is desired to build on the edge of the alpine village Vals that is 1200 meters above the sea level. Its guests are expected to be rich alpine skiers from Asia and Middle East. About 100 rooms will be at their disposal. Cost of the cheapest room will be 1000 swiss francs (about 1000 dollars) per night, the most expensive one is 25 thousand. They have already made a financial plan. The highest building of the Old World will cost the ordering party, Remo Staffel, Vals ski resort owner, and the swiss businessman Paies Truffer, approx. 200 mil francs. The financial plan is, of course, preliminary and it is to be exceeded while building.

Bullets:

  • If the project is technically realized, it will be incredibly interesting.
  • "Aired Tower" (nonoficial name of the project) is considered to have a fully glassed facade. It is an exremely difficult task for design engineers: they have to consider the increased wind pressures, avalanching, snow load, possible icing, not to mention earthquake risks.
  • These factors demand special requirements of applied construction elements, including sealers, plastic profiles and glasses - the latter, for example, should comply with wind pressure requirements and have energy-saving functions. In general, energy-saving matter is one of cornerstones (as well as water and sewage systems supply), if this project is realised, it would be interesting, firstly, due to the integration of all modern technologies of energy-saving: beginning from solar batteries (usage solar radiation in mountains is much more effective) and wind motors to earth energy production (through soil temperature changes).
